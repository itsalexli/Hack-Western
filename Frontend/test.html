Critical illness insurance – simplified view

Money to help if a serious illness changes your life

Critical illness insurance can give you a tax-free lump-sum payment if you’re diagnosed with a covered condition and meet the policy requirements. You can use this money however you need – from everyday bills to extra medical costs.

What is critical illness insurance?

It’s insurance that pays you a one-time lump sum if you’re diagnosed with a covered serious illness (for example, certain types of cancer, heart attack, or stroke), and you meet the policy’s conditions and survival period.

Important: The money is paid to you, not to a hospital. You decide how to use it.

You might use the benefit to:

  • Replace lost income while you take time away from work.
  • Pay rent, mortgage, or other everyday living expenses.
  • Cover travel, parking, or accommodation for treatment.
  • Pay for services that make life easier, like childcare or home support.

Who might this coverage help?

This type of insurance may be helpful if one or more of these sounds like you:

You rely on a paycheque
You have a mortgage or rent
You support children or family
You want help with extra medical costs
You’re self-employed

Key features at a glance

  • Lump-sum payment: A single tax-free amount if you’re diagnosed with a covered condition and survive the required period.
  • You choose how to use it: Health expenses, household bills, debt payments, or anything else.
  • Coverage for many serious conditions: Policies can cover a range of major illnesses. Exact conditions depend on the specific plan you choose.
  • Options for adults and children: Some plans offer coverage for children as well as adults.
  • Optional add-ons: Certain plans may offer extras such as return of premium or coverage that continues if you become disabled (details vary by product).

How it generally works

  1. You apply for coverage.
    You choose a coverage amount and answer questions about your health and lifestyle, depending on the product.
  2. You pay regular premiums.
    Premiums may be monthly or yearly.
  3. A covered illness is diagnosed.
    If you’re diagnosed with a covered condition as defined in your policy, your advisor can help you submit a claim.
  4. Benefit paid if your claim is approved.
    Once you satisfy any waiting or survival periods, the lump sum is paid, subject to the terms and exclusions in your contract.
Things to review carefully in your policy
  • The exact illnesses and definitions that are covered.
  • Any age limits, term lengths, and renewal rules.
  • Waiting periods and required survival periods after diagnosis.
  • Exclusions (for example, pre-existing conditions or certain situations).
  • What happens if you cancel your policy or stop paying premiums.
